My research combines Earth system modelling, marine biogeochemistry, and sediment diagenesis to understand how interactions between the ocean, sediments, and climate regulate Earth's carbon cycle across timescales ranging from decades to millions of years.

---

## Marine Organic Carbon Burial and Sediment Biogeochemistry

Marine sediments play a central role in regulating Earth's climate by storing organic carbon and recycling nutrients back to the ocean. My research combines global datasets with numerical early diagenetic models to quantify controls on organic carbon preservation, nutrient regeneration, and greenhouse gas production in marine sediments.

Current research topics include:

- Global marine organic carbon burial
- Early diagenetic modelling (OMEN-SED)
- Carbonate diagenesis
- Nutrient regeneration and benthic fluxes
- Coastal blue carbon
- Continental margin carbon cycling

---

## Earth System Modelling and Past Climate Change

I use an intermediate-complexity Earth system model to investigate how feedbacks between climate, ocean circulation, marine biogeochemistry, and sediments have shaped Earth's climate throughout geological history.

Current research topics include:

- Hyperthermal recovery and carbon cycle feedbacks
- Ocean deoxygenation and anoxia
- Earth system resilience and climate recovery
